I volunteered with UNICEF Netherlands for its Jordan project which is running currently.Through this project UNICEF is providing access to education to migrant children in Jordan.We as volunteers, our task was to make poeple aware of this project and raise funds.

I was selected for the SMILE(Student Mobilisation Initiative for Learning through Exposure) internship of Pravah in 2019.Pravah is a Delhi based organisation which works with young people to impact issues of social justice through youth citizenship action . In this internship, we had a 5 day camp and then we were sent to different organisation to work with them. I was sent to Manthan Kotri,Rajasthan; this organisation work on water conservation. I had to learn about one of their community and had to write a report about what did I see and learn from the community.I also helped the organisation to conduct their annual meet with the fellows who were at Manthan earlier and to translate their work into English.

I was the Team Manager and fund mobiliser of Team Udayan (Sponsored by NGO Udayan centre for Collaborative Learning). I managed a Team of four walkers in terms of their training schedules; diet; logistics; planning; material support and constant field back up for the continues 33 hours the team was on the road. Two of the team members finished the walk successfully while another did 50 kilometers. The walk being a fund raiser I could bring the team in the top 5 teams mobilising over 2Million Rupees through crowd funding. Team was given a special recognition for this.

Young Leaders for Active Citizenship (YLAC) is an initiative to build capacity of youngsters through active citizenship on issues like Diversity, Mental wellness-being, Sustainability,Gender Equality, Body Positivity and Bullying. It is driven by eminent citizens like Shashi Tharoor, Jay Panda, and a group of Harvard and Oxford Alumni. I was privileged to have been selected as one of the 30 Counter Speech Fellows after a rigorous selection process from across the country. I was rated as one of the best performers consistently by peers.

Aashish Centre is a vocational school for differently-abled children. The school trained them with some life skills and vocational training. I volunteered at Aashish center during the summer break between class 10 and high school. At the center, I learnt from the facilitators and assisted them with organizing classes and activities. For example, I arranged the individual timetables for each student so that they could keep track of their tasks for the day. I helped conduct activities like colour differentiation, movement exercises for psycho motor skills, and visits to public places to train social interaction skills. I learnt how children on the autistic spectrum have difficulty doing routine tasks and interactions, and that as a society we can do better at accepting those different from us.
